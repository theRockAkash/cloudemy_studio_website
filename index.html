Features

Everything Educators Need to Record Professional Video Lectures

Cloudemy Studio combines selective screen recording, powerful annotation tools, and advanced video settings into one app designed specifically for teachers and content creators.

Selective Screen Recording

Unlike regular screen recorders, Cloudemy Studio captures only your presentation content. The status bar, navigation buttons, and other system UI elements are automatically excluded, giving your video lectures a clean, professional appearance that keeps students focused on your teaching material.

PDF and PowerPoint Import

Load your existing PDF documents and PowerPoint presentations directly into the app. Your slides are converted and ready for annotation immediately, so you can start recording video lectures within seconds of opening the app. No file conversion or third-party tools required.

Powerful Drawing and Annotation

Write and draw directly on slides using your finger or stylus. Choose from pens, shapes, arrows, and pointer tools with unlimited color options. Highlight key concepts, draw diagrams, solve equations, and create visual explanations that help students understand complex topics.

Slide Grid View and Management

Rearrange your slides effortlessly with the intuitive grid view. Reorder your presentation flow, delete unnecessary slides, and organize your lecture material before you start recording. This visual overview makes lesson preparation fast and straightforward.

Customizable Slide Backgrounds

Choose any background color from a full spectrum color picker to match your lesson themes. Whether you need a dark background for handwriting, white for text-heavy content, or a custom color for branding, Cloudemy Studio gives you complete control over your visual presentation.

Easy Export and Sharing

Export your projects as .cslide files to share with colleagues who can continue recording on their own device. Download drawn slides as images or PDFs for student review materials. Share finished video lectures directly to YouTube or any platform your students use.
